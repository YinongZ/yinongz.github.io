[Reading]

Bojana Coklyat & Shannon Finnegan - Selections from Alt-Text as Poetry Workbook

[Response]

The article's approach to alt-text as a creative practice rather than just a functional tool is refreshing. I appreciate how it challenges the myth of "objectivity" in description by framing alt-text as both translation and poetry. The argument that describing images is inherently subjective resonates with me. The example asking whether to write "smile" or "lips turned up at the corners" perfectly illustrates how all description involves interpretation. No description is truly neutral. The three poetic qualities they highlight—attention to language, word economy, and experimental spirit—provide a useful framework for writing more effective and engaging alt-text. This perspective acknowledges that accessibility content can have style and personality while still serving its purpose. I found their discussion of artists writing alt-text for their own work particularly interesting—balancing the insight artists bring about their process against the tendency to describe intent rather than what's actually visible.

Questions:

How might the concept of "alt-text as poetry" evolve as AI-generated descriptions become more common? Could AI capture the subjective, creative aspects the authors value?

How could websites implement attribution for alt-text authors without creating confusion for screen reader users?
